Privacy Policy

Effective date: 8 June 2026  ·  Amplifi Group Holdings Ltd  ·  Company No. 17094221

What Amplifi Spark is

Amplifi Spark is a cashback and family gifting platform. We are not an investment platform and we do not hold, manage, or invest your money. We help families direct cashback earnings and contributions from friends and family into a Junior ISA held at a provider of their choice.

Amplifi Group Holdings Ltd is the data controller for all personal data processed through this website and the Amplifi Spark service.

What data we collect

We collect the following personal data when you sign up and use Amplifi Spark:

  • Your name and email address
  • Your child's name and date of birth (to personalise the service)
  • Your child's Junior ISA sort code and account number (to direct contributions)
  • Payment handles you choose to connect — such as Monzo, PayPal, or Revolut usernames
  • Referral codes you generate or use
  • Transaction and cashback data related to your activity on the platform
  • Basic usage analytics — pages visited, features used, session duration

We do not collect data from children directly. All data about a child is provided by a parent or guardian.

Why we collect it

We use your data only to run the service:

  • To create and manage your account
  • To process cashback earnings and direct them to the correct Junior ISA
  • To enable family gifting — so grandparents, aunts, uncles, and friends can contribute
  • To prevent fraud and comply with financial regulations
  • To improve the platform based on how it is used
  • To contact you about your account or the service (no marketing without your consent)

Our legal basis for processing is contract performance (to provide the service you signed up for), legitimate interests (fraud prevention, service improvement), and consent where required.

Children's data

We take extra care with children's data. A child's name and date of birth are used only to personalise their pot on the platform. We never share this data with third parties for marketing purposes, and we never use it for profiling or advertising.

If you believe we hold data about a child without appropriate parental consent, please contact us immediately at hello@letsamplifi.com and we will delete it promptly.

How your data is stored

Your data is stored in Supabase, hosted in the EU (London region). All data is encrypted at rest and in transit using industry-standard TLS. We do not store payment card numbers — payments are handled entirely by Stripe.

Who we share data with

We only share data with third parties where it is necessary to run the service. We do not sell your data.

  • Supabase — our database provider (EU, London)
  • Netlify — our hosting provider
  • Stripe — payment processing
  • PostHog — anonymised usage analytics (EU hosted; no personally identifiable data is sent)

The following integrations are planned but not yet active. Data will only be shared with them once those features go live, and you will be notified:

  • Tillo — gift card processing
  • Fidel — card-linked cashback offers
  • Sientia — open banking

All third-party processors are contractually required to protect your data and may only use it for the specific purpose we instruct.

Cookies

We use cookies only for what is strictly necessary: keeping you logged in during a session. We do not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or third-party analytics cookies that identify you personally. PostHog analytics are anonymised and session-based.

Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights over your personal data:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Correction — ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Deletion — ask us to delete your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Object — object to us processing your data on the basis of legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, email us at hello@letsamplifi.com. We will respond within 30 days.

If you are unhappy with how we handle your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

How long we keep your data

We keep your account data for as long as your account is active. If you close your account, we will delete your personal data within 90 days, except where we are required to retain it by law (for example, for fraud prevention or financial compliance purposes).

Updates to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The effective date at the top of this page will always show when it was last changed. If we make material changes, we will notify you by email.
